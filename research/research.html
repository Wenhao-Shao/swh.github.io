Research Interest

1. Circular and Hierarchical Molecular Design

  • Read More

The conceptual key to our approach is bridging multi-level structure-function relationships to achieve hierarchical and circular design rationale for the ultimate new and complex material systems. In particular, circular design accelerates and guides molecular space search by crystal structure prediction, automated molecular generator, and first-principle screening. On the other hand, hierarchical design escalates and converges molecular functionality from molecular-level interactions, crystal structures, morphologies, and ultimately lead to advanced self-assembled purely organic and hybrid systems with superior photonic.

Designing organic building blocks and understanding their structure-functional relationships are crucial in modern electronic, photonic, and bio-integrated systems due to their low-cost fabrication and vast chemical space. The increasing design complexity from purely organic materials to hybrid crystals and interfaces necessitates a solid connection between structures, molecular orbital manipulation, self-assembly, electronic, and luminescent properties.
